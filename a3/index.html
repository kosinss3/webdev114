You should know about water.

Even though 70% of the earth is covered in water (there is an astonishing 332.5 million cubic miles of water on Earth), only about 2.5% of that water is fresh water. Of that fresh water only about 1% of it is easily accessible. Most of the world's fresh water is either trapped underground, or frozen in glaciers. Despite the fact that only a small amount of the water in the world is fresh water and is easily accessible there is still enough water to sustain a multitude of life.

Water is so ubiquitous that oftentimes we neglet to give it a fair share of our attention, perhaps it's part of human nature to take things for granted. It is estimated that as much as 50% of people don't drink enough water on a daily basis. Dehydration is a common cause for many common daily health issues, such as dizziness, fainting, headaches, rapid heartrate, dry skin, fatigue, and confusion. Recognizing when your body is thirsty is the key to adequate hydration. There are many signs, including the previously listed symptoms, along with things like yellow urine, and dry mouth. When your body is thirsty it's time to drink some water!

Benefits of drinking water:

  • Regulates body temperature
  • Healthier skin
  • Lubricates joints
  • Maintains blood pressure
  • Prevents kidney damage
  • Helps with mineral uptake
  • Forms saliva
  • Cushions the brain
  • Circulates oxygen
  • Aids digestion
  • Helps respiration
  • Reduces hangovers
  • Increases athletic performance
  • Assists with weight loss

Stay hydrated, stay healthy!

It's easy to see why staying hydrated is important, and luckily it's one of the easiest things you can do to boost your health. Just pour yourself a glass of nice refreshing water any time you're feeling thirsty. It's that simple. You won't regret it. Cheers!
