Alocasia Amazonica

Alocasia, like many commonly kept house plants is a tropical plant that grows on the rainforest floor. This allows them to thrive in bright indirect sunlight. The leaves can grow to almost 3 feet long. Alocasia bears a small flower.

Alocasia Amazonica has striking, colorful foliage. Some Alocasia grow to be quite large, however this one is less than a foot tall. It currently has three new leaves that have been unfurling for close to a month now. I'm not sure if it's supposed to take that long or if I should find a brighter home for it. Anyway, I'll make that decision another time.

Despite the name "Amazonica", Alocasia is actually native to the tropics of Asia and Oceania. It got the name because it is actually a human-cultivated hybrid plant. This has probably led to its popularity as a house plant.
